Our mission
We do more than meet a need. We create the space to imagine what comes next.

The House of Manna is a faith-inspired public charity built around a simple truth: hunger consumes attention. When families receive dignified support, they gain the freedom to think beyond the next meal, young people gain room to prepare for their futures, and communities gain the strength to grow.

Food support is the beginning—not the boundary—of our work.

Strategic growth phases

Built to grow with discipline.

House of Manna moves from curriculum and pantry launch to bi-weekly distributions, mobile outreach, and market expansion.

01

Academy Curriculum

Develop the Academy curriculum and establish school and youth-serving partnerships.

02

Scholarships + Partnerships

Launch entrepreneurship scholarships while expanding community and funding partnerships.

03

Pantry Launch

Begin pantry operations with an initial goal of serving at least 50 families each month.

04

Community Expansion

Grow sponsorships, volunteers, churches, schools, and nonprofit relationships.

05

Bi-weekly + Mobile

Increase distribution frequency and begin mobile outreach to communities with the greatest need.

06

Market Expansion

Extend the model into the Glen Burnie and Chicago markets with local partners.

Baltimore community focus

Targeting need with intention.

Our outreach sequence will be driven by a transparent, need-based assessment—not convenience alone. We will prioritize neighborhoods where food insecurity intersects with child poverty, transportation barriers, and limited access to healthy food.

01
Family need

Concentrations of food insecurity, poverty, and housing instability.

02
Access barriers

Distance from healthy food, transportation limitations, and uneven retail access.

03
Youth opportunity

Communities where education, career exposure, and scholarships can create additional leverage.

04
Local partnership capacity

Trusted organizations that can help us serve consistently and responsibly.

How we measure success

Not simply by what we distribute—but by what becomes possible.

Transformation can look like a family supported through a difficult season, a student discovering a future career, a volunteer finding purpose, or a neighborhood becoming stronger because House of Manna was present.
